ICS Cybersecurity Simulation Testbed

Designed and implemented a full virtual ICS/SCADA environment using Docker, OpenPLC, ScadaBR, Python, Modbus/TCP, and Linux to simulate real industrial processes, PLC logic, and operator HMIs without any hardware. Built and executed realistic offensive scenarios including MITM on Modbus, unauthorized coil/register writes, PLC and SCADA compromise, and cyber-physical process manipulation then engineered defenses with Purdue-model network segmentation, Grafana–Loki–Promtail observability, and ML-based anomaly detection with real-time alerting.

This project shows my ability to understand, simulate, attack, and secure industrial control systems end-to-end, giving enterprises a safe cyber-range to train blue/red teams, test SOC detection rules, and evaluate ICS security controls before deploying them in production.

ULB SIEM & Log Pipeline Internship

Worked as a Cybersecurity & SIEM intern in the IT Security department at Université Libre de Bruxelles (ULB), improving the university’s EFK-based SIEM. Designed, tuned, and documented Fluent Bit/Vector pipelines and Lua/YAML parsers to collect and normalize Microsoft 365, CAS authentication, Palo Alto firewall, and internal infrastructure logs into Elasticsearch.

Strengthened SOC visibility and threat detection by building Kibana data views and dashboards, testing migration from Fluent Bit to Vector, and securing access with Vault-based short-lived credentials reducing monitoring blind spots, accelerating incident investigations, and making the SIEM architecture more maintainable for future engineers.
